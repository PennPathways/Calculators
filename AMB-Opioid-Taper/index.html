Terminal taper switch
ER to IR conversion guidance

Convert the patient from ER to IR when the taper hits the lowest manufactured dose of the ER medication and further reductions are impossible — ER tablets cannot be cut.

Lowest available ER strengths

  • Oxycodone ER (OxyContin): Lowest dose is 10 mg (taken Q12h = 20 mg total daily dose)
  • Morphine ER (MS Contin): Lowest dose is 15 mg (taken Q12h = 30 mg total daily dose)

How the terminal switch works

Once a patient is stable on Oxycodone ER 10 mg twice daily (20 mg total daily dose) and is ready to step down further, you cannot prescribe a "5 mg ER" tablet because it doesn't exist — and you cannot cut the 10 mg ER tablet.

At this exact crossroad, convert the 20 mg total daily dose into an IR regimen:

  1. Switch to Oxycodone IR 5 mg taken three to four times a day (maintaining a similar total daily dose of 15–20 mg).
  2. Allow the patient to stabilize on the IR regimen for 1–2 weeks.
  3. Begin tapering the IR regimen by either extending the time between doses (e.g., from every 6 hours to every 8 hours) or using a pill cutter to halve the IR tablets (e.g., dropping a 5 mg IR tablet to 2.5 mg).
